Act as the security operations engineer responding to a failed DNS zone-failover rollout. The failover automation left persistence and escalation paths on the resolver host: a rogue automation key in root's `authorized_keys`, a passwordless sudoers drop-in, and a world-readable TSIG zone-transfer key. First contain the host to match `/app/docs/containment_runbook.md` — revoke the automation's SSH persistence, remove its privilege escalation, and lock down the exposed key.

Then restore `/app/workflow/export_report.py`, the control-plane reconciler that converts authoritative-zone drift alerts into responder containment work. Preserve its operational CLI: `--input PATH` and `--output-dir PATH`, defaulting to `/app/data/events.json` and `/app/output`.

This is security-control reconciliation, not application feature development. The resulting artifacts decide which zone drifts require immediate containment, which are attenuated by approved policy windows, and how risk carries between repeated drifts in the same environment.

Always read freezes and scoped attenuation policies from their fixed absolute paths: `/app/data/change_freezes.json`, `/app/data/reopen_windows.json`, `/app/data/rotation_windows.json`, `/app/data/defer_windows.json`, and `/app/data/zone_delegation_edges.json`. `--input` changes only the event stream; policy files never become relative to input/output directories.

The output contract — the exact file paths, schemas, required fields, repair tokens, digest payloads, and byte-level checksum serialization for the three artifacts — is written in `/app/docs/report_spec.json`. The reconciler's behaviour is not in that contract, and you have to reconstruct it: canonicalization and dedupe of the raw alerts, drift-window merging, the four scoped attenuation layers, the per-environment risk ledger, the probe and volatility scoring, the zone-delegation trust-exposure score, and the queue admission, priority and ordering rules. The governing values behind every one of those stages were settled by the failover governance board and recorded in `/app/incident/failover_review_log.md`; several early positions were later revised, and where an earlier ruling conflicts with a later dated one, the later ruling governs.

Write `summary.json`, `drift_windows.json`, and a compact `response_queue.jsonl` under the requested output directory. Every emitted value must derive from the operational inputs and the governing decisions; the reconciler must produce identical output on reruns and keep working when pointed at a different alert stream via `--input`. Keep the frozen incident snapshot `/app/workflow/.export_report.original` unchanged.
